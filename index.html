Henri Nestlé

Founder of Nestle and therefore white chocolate

  • 1814: Born Heinrich Nestle in Frankfurt am Main, Germany

  • 1834: Completed four-year apprenticeship with J.E Stein, owner of a Frankfurt pharmacy

  • 1834-1839: Migrated to Switzerland

  • 1839: Authorized in Lausanne, Switzerland, to perform chemical experiments, make up prescriptions and sell medicines and changes name to Herni Nestlé

  • 1843: Bought into one of the region's production of rapeseeds and became involved in producing nut oils, liqueurs, rum, absinthe and vinegar

  • 1860: Married Anna Clémentine Thérèse Ehemant in Frankfurt, Germany

  • 1867: Produced a viable powdered milk product, which became a viable option for infants who were unable to breastfeed

  • 1870's: Nestlé's Infant Food, which was made with malt, cow's milk, sugar and wheat flour, was selling in the US for $0.50 a bottle

  • 1879: Helped chocolatier Daniel Peter perfect his milk chocolate formulation and the organization of the Nestlé Company

  • 1890: Died of a heart attack
